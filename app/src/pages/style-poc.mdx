  title="Master and Daily Schedules"
  meta="Beginner · ~10 minutes · Self-paced"
/>

OpCon separates the *definition* of a workflow from a *specific day's run* of that workflow. The definition lives in the **Master** tables; the day-by-day instances live in the **Daily** tables. Understanding this split is the foundation for everything else — frequencies, builds, reruns, and reporting all hinge on it.

<Section number="1" title="The Master / Daily split" />

## What lives where

The Master schedule is the template. It contains every job, dependency, frequency, and event the schedule could ever need. Editing it changes future days, not the day already in flight.

The Daily schedule is what runs. Each calendar date that the schedule is built for produces its own Daily instance — a snapshot of the Master frozen at build time, with statuses and history attached as the day progresses.

## Why the split exists

Without it, an edit to a job would either retroactively rewrite history (breaking auditability) or be impossible to make until every running schedule completed (breaking agility). The Master/Daily split lets you change tomorrow's run while leaving today's untouched.

<WhyItMatters>
  When operations reports a problem with "yesterday's run," they mean the Daily instance, not the Master. Status changes, reruns, and overrides happen on the Daily. Configuration changes happen on the Master and take effect on the *next* build.
</WhyItMatters>

<Section number="2" title="The Build process" />

## How a Daily gets created

Each schedule runs through a build at a configured time (typically the prior evening). The build engine reads the Master, applies the active frequencies for the target date, resolves calendars and dependencies, and writes a fresh Daily instance.

Once built, the Daily is independent. Edits to the Master after the build are not reflected until the next build for that date — which is why a "rebuild" exists as an explicit operation.

<Section number="3" title="Three things to remember" />

<Takeaways>
  <Takeaway number="1" title="Master = definition">
    Edits to the Master affect future builds, never a Daily that has already been built.
  </Takeaway>
  <Takeaway number="2" title="Daily = execution">
    Status changes, reruns, comments, and history all live on the Daily.
  </Takeaway>
  <Takeaway number="3" title="Build = the bridge">
    The build process snapshots the Master into a Daily for a specific date. Re-run a build to pick up Master changes.
  </Takeaway>
</Takeaways>

<WhyItMatters label="Connecting to your work:">
  Almost every "OpCon did the wrong thing" ticket comes down to confusion between the Master and the Daily. When you can quickly identify which one a customer is looking at, you've already done half the diagnostic work.
</WhyItMatters>
